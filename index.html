Formula One (1950 - 2023)

Formula One (F1) is one of the world's most prestigious racing competitions. Races take place in multiple locations around the world with millions of viewers and various sponsors participating. Each team engineers their own F1 car in which their drivers race around a circuit. Lets see how the fastest lap times differ between different circuits and where do they tend to be hosted?

F1 Circuit Locations

The following map illustrates from 1950 to 2023, the locations where all F1 circuits have been held as well as the number of times it has been held there. The countries with a darker orange colour have held more circuits than those with a lighter orange colour.

For example, we can see that Canada has held a total of 42 events.

We focus on the top 7 countries with the most events, including Australia and Malaysia as main points of references.

Fastest Lap Times

How are the fastest lap times improving over the years? The line chart shows the trend of the fastest lap times and their corresponding circuit over the years. To filter a specific circuit location, HOVER and CLICK the drop-menu, SELECT the circuit of interest.

Note that countries such as China and Russia are missing some data years, indicating that they no longer host F1 races.

Meanwhile, we can see that Australia, United Kingdom and Japan have consistently held F1 races in their respective country.

Top 10 Fastest Circuits

The bar chart on the left shows the top 10 fastest circuits overall by considering all the countries. Here, we can see that a circuit in Bahrain holds the fastest lap time ever, with a lap time of 55 seconds in 2020. For older years, a slower fastest lap time would be expected, however we can see that a circuit in Argentina has competed fairly well against circuit lap times in Malaysia and Russia.
